About SquadUp

We built the app
we couldn't find.

A story about five games, seventeen apps, and one very obvious problem nobody had bothered to solve.

🎮
2021 — The Problem

It started with a bad game of Valorant.

Four of us were trying to coordinate a ranked push. One teammate was on Discord. Another was DMing on Instagram. We had a group chat on Telegram for scheduling. Our streams were on Twitch. Our highlight clips were on YouTube. We lost the match — not because we played badly, but because someone missed the callout while switching between tabs.

That night, our founder Aryan counted the apps he had open just to play games with friends: Discord, Telegram, Instagram, Twitch, YouTube, Steam, and a spreadsheet to track schedules. Seven apps. To play one game. With people he already knew.

It was absurd. And everyone we talked to had the same problem.

"The gaming world had incredible tools — just none of them talked to each other. We were duct-taping five apps together and calling it a social experience."
— Aryan Shah, Co-founder
💡
2022 — The Idea

What if gamers had one home?

The idea wasn't radical. In fact, it felt embarrassingly obvious in retrospect. Gamers needed a single place where they could chat with their squad, stream to their community, share their plays, plan their sessions, and discover new teammates — all without leaving the platform.

Aryan teamed up with two engineers he'd met on a Valorant Discord server (fittingly) — Priya and Rajan — and they started building in Priya's apartment. No office. No funding. Just a shared conviction that the gaming social experience was broken, and the skills to fix it.

They called it SquadUp. Because that's what gamers do.

Jan 2022

First prototype

A basic chat app with profile cards. Shared it with 20 friends in their gaming circles.

May 2022

Beta launch

Added live streaming and squad matching. 500 beta users joined the first weekend.

Oct 2022

Story Feed

Built the clip-sharing feed. Within weeks, creators were posting daily gaming moments.

Mar 2023

10,000 users

Grew entirely through word of mouth. No ads. No influencers. Just squads telling squads.

2024

80,000+ gamers

SquadUp becomes the home for competitive gaming communities across 340+ games.

Our Values

Built different, by design.

We've made a few promises to ourselves — and to our users — that guide every decision we make:

🎯

Gamers first, always

We don't build for advertisers, investors, or engagement metrics. We build for the person who just wants to play with their friends without seventeen apps open.

⚡

Performance is a feature

A laggy platform is a broken platform. We obsess over latency, reliability, and speed — because 32ms vs 200ms is the difference between a win and a loss.

🤝

Community over clout

We don't want SquadUp to become another place to perform for an algorithm. We're building for real squads, real friendships, and real games played together.

🔒

Your data is yours

We will never sell your data, never show you ads based on your conversations, and never build a business model that treats you as a product.

🚀
What's next

We're just getting started.

SquadUp today is version one of a much longer vision. We're building toward a world where every gamer — regardless of platform, game, or time zone — can find their people, play their best, and share those moments without friction.

The early access list is open. Be among the first to help us shape what comes next.
